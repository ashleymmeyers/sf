Before we get started, you'll need:

  1. Your current California DMV registration, in your name and at this permit address.
  2. Your current vehicle insurance policy/declaration page, in your name and at this permit address.
  3. Visa, MasterCard, or American Express to pay online.
  4. To pay any outstanding parking citations. You can check and pay them here.

If the vehicle you wish to get a permit for is not registered and insured under your name and this address, you may still be eligible for a permit. You will have to fill out a paper application. See the Special Exceptions section of this page for more details.

Special Exceptions

If your vehicle is registered elsewhere or insured to another name or address, you may still be eligible. For example, this may be true if you are:

  • Active military personnel
  • A full-time student at a higher education institution in San Francisco
  • Parking a business-owned vehicle at home
  • A foreign consulate
  • A medical or child caregiver

If you have a new or recently purchased vehicle, you can receive a temporary permit until you have proof of registration and insurance.

If one of the above applies to you, please read detailed instructions and download a printable application form here.
